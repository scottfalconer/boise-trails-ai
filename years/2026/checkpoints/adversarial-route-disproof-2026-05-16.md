# Adversarial Route Disproof Review

Date: 2026-05-16

Purpose: try to disprove the current 2026 route set by attacking the routes that still look weird after the route-review gate: high overhead, high ratio, declared repeat, and same-trailhead bundles.

This is not a global optimum proof. It is a local proof ledger: under the current accepted anchors, generated candidate universe, GPX continuity checks, p75 timing, DEM effort, boundary recombination audits, and global optimizer challenge, I did not find a replacement that is both better and more field-ready than the current packet.

## Review Frames

- Accepted-anchor dominance: FD14D-class mistakes are blocked by the route-review gate. No deterministic same-credit dominance remains in the current all-route pack.
- Same-trailhead bundle challenge: the repeat audit still reports same-trailhead warnings, but none is currently proven to reduce human footmiles while preserving exact credit, GPX continuity, p75 timing, and route-card readiness.
- Boundary recombination challenge: the existing boundary challenges cover the high-overhead boundaries and report no dominant generated replacement with p75 and DEM metrics.
- Global set-cover challenge: the executable candidate universe reports zero dominant whole-plan replacements.
- Skeptical hiker challenge: several routes are still annoying, but the annoyance is currently explained by single-car access, direction/ascent rules, mountain access, or the lack of a certified better start.
