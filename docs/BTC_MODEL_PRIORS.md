# BTC Model Priors

Use these as the compressed project priors behind the heuristics.

## Core Prior

BTC is edge coverage under human constraints.

Official challenge credit is about completing required trail segment geometry in the right mode and direction. The useful product is a field-executable, car-to-car outing menu that can be recertified as official data, closures, access, and completed segments change.

## Operational Priors

- Trailheads are access hypotheses, not completion objects.
- A GPX line is not enough; field use needs signpost-oriented cueing from the car back to the car.
- Route quality depends on p75 door-to-door time, DEM effort, heat, water, bailout, parking, and hard stops.
- Split routes are valid when they improve timing, heat risk, bailout, or family/work logistics.
- Completed segments may remain physically necessary as repeat mileage or connector context.
- Current official 2026 data beats archived 2025 planner artifacts for official truth.
- Land-manager special-management rules are separate field-readiness truth from BTC official `direction`/`ascent` fields; a route can satisfy challenge segment math and still be illegal or socially unsafe to publish.
- Route-experience, audit, and optimizer artifacts are upstream review inputs until promoted into the canonical field-menu source.

## Skill Boundary

AGENTS.md carries standing rules and hard invariants. Repo-local skills in `.agents/skills/` carry repeatable procedures. A route can still be wrong if a hard invariant is absent from a skill invocation, so durable route, proof, artifact-source, and privacy contracts remain in AGENTS.md.
