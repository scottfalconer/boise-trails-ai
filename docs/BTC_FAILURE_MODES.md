# BTC Failure Modes

Failure modes are recurring mistakes the heuristics are meant to prevent. They should stay concrete enough to connect to observed planner behavior, field tests, or artifact audits.

| ID | Failure mode | Preventing heuristic | Mitigation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `btc_failure_edge_001` | Required trails are treated like points, trailheads, or names rather than official segment edges. | Edge-not-point reasoning | Build the required edge set first, then validate endpoint-to-endpoint coverage and ascent direction. |
| `btc_failure_graph_002` | A one-car route is decomposed into trailhead-anchored segment-cluster excursions instead of one closed required-edge tour. | Closed edge-cover route cards | Treat the selected trailhead as the fixed depot, clear required spurs from junctions before continuing, and fail or advisory-flag phase-reset returns before all required edges are cleared. |
| `btc_failure_access_001` | A mapped trailhead, pullout, OSM parking node, or road crossing is treated as guaranteed legal and practical access. | Trailhead Affordance Check | Verify parking, road passability, gates, signage, closure, private-property ambiguity, and public-safe labels before promotion. |
| `btc_failure_access_002` | The planner optimizes to the nearest road or residential edge and misses a slightly farther certifiable park, trailhead, or public parking surface. | Certifiable parking before closest road | Search outward for legal, repeatable parking, price the connector honestly, and compare the certifiable route against the baseline before rejecting the idea. |
| `btc_failure_legality_001` | BTC official segment/ascent validation is treated as sufficient even though the route violates a published land-manager direction, date/use, or mode rule. | Published trail-management rules are certification inputs | Check data-backed special-management rules against the actual GPX traversal, including connector/repeat mileage, and block the route until redesigned or source-proven compliant. |
| `btc_failure_field_001` | A GPX or route line is treated as field-ready without car-to-car wayfinding cues. | GPX-valid is not human-valid | Require signed access, connector, repeat, road, return, and caution cues from the parked car back to the parked car. |
| `btc_failure_field_002` | A cue sequence is treated as runnable even though cue mileage, GPX/map span, and parked-car passes describe different physical movement. | GPX-valid is not human-valid | Hold the route as `blocked_navigation_source`, repair the canonical route topology or split, regenerate GPX/cues/field days together, and rerun certification. |
| `btc_failure_field_003` | Scaled phone cue mileage matches the card, but live-map route anchors reveal materially longer movement caused by repeat/out-and-back geometry. | One route truth | Fail field-tool completion until route-card mileage, phone cue mileage, and live-map route anchors reconcile from one canonical car-to-car route. |
| `btc_failure_field_004` | The live map opens on cue 2 or later because a zero-length start cue shares the same route-mile anchor as the first movement leg. | GPX-valid is not human-valid | Fail field-tool completion until the generator suppresses/merges the zero-length start cue or renders it as a start marker without making the first active leg skip the field-visible start. |
| `btc_failure_field_005` | A natural loop is split into package ownership buckets, so the exported card repeats the wrong side of the loop while the natural return trail is assigned elsewhere. | One route truth | Recombine the official edges into the human-recognizable car-to-car loop or hold the route as not field-ready; never let package ownership override the field route shape. |
| `btc_failure_artifact_001` | Map, phone packet, GPX, written menu, and source data drift into different route truths. | One route truth | Fix the canonical source or exporter, regenerate downstream artifacts, and re-run certification checks. |
| `btc_failure_artifact_002` | A promoted replacement or source-selection rule is not preserved, so a packet resurrects stale cards, falls back to sanitized/partial data, or produces false route-card failures. | One route truth | Compare canonical source hashes and explicit map-data paths before debugging route logic; preserve accepted replacements or explicitly supersede them, then regenerate all field artifacts from one source. |
| `btc_failure_artifact_003` | A segment-ownership promotion keeps deleting a source card after its supporting evidence artifact or regenerated source route no longer proves the ownership move. | One route truth | Re-run the promotion evidence gate against current generated source before suppressing the old card; if it fails, mark the promotion `retracted`, restore the source card, and recertify. |
| `btc_failure_artifact_004` | The canonical outing menu, public map, and field packet share segment ids but disagree on route mileage, p75, cue metadata, or connector-savings proof. | One route truth | Compare field-packet route records to canonical outing-menu components and compare cue metadata to current connector proof; block publication until all field artifacts regenerate from the same source truth. |
| `btc_failure_cost_001` | Routes are ranked by graph distance, official miles, or fewer starts while ignoring field cost. | Runnable cost, not map cost | Use p75 door-to-door timing, DEM effort, drive/prep/transfer time, heat, water, bailout, and hard-stop risk. |
| `btc_failure_cost_002` | Accepted multi-start or same-day re-park routes are treated as private/manual overrides and disappear during active recalculation. | Runnable cost, not map cost | Make split/re-park candidates first-class generated replacements selected by runnable-cost rules and re-run certification after recalculation. |
| `btc_failure_schedule_001` | Weekend days are assumed to have more route capacity than weekdays, so routes are forced, rejected, or promoted from day type instead of actual availability. | Date-specific availability, not weekday/weekend proxy | Use explicit dated availability windows and hard stops; keep weekday/weekend labels informational unless a current personal profile says otherwise for that exact date. |
| `btc_failure_promotion_001` | Optimizer or multi-start savings are promoted before the route is field-certified. | Certification before promotion | Keep candidates in checkpoint/research state until source, GPX, cues, p75/p90, access, direction, and audits agree. |
| `btc_failure_evidence_001` | Strava, archived code, stale imagery, or old planner assumptions are treated as current official truth. | Evidence scope discipline | State evidence scope and use current-year official data for official segment truth. |
| `btc_failure_progress_001` | Phone checkboxes, partial overlaps, crossings, or multi-activity fragments are counted as challenge progress. | Full-segment credit before progress | Validate one on-foot activity against full official segment geometry and ascent direction before marking progress. |
| `btc_failure_progress_002` | Progress is applied outing-first, the original route baseline is overwritten, or extra side/spur segments are lost during recalculation. | Full-segment credit before progress | Use a segment-first private ledger, derive completed outings from completed segment sets, lock epoch originals, and version each active recalculation. |
| `btc_failure_progress_003` | A route GPX completes unclaimed official segments but the field menu leaves those segments assigned to a later active route. | Full-segment credit before progress | Run a cross-route latent-credit audit, then claim the segment, mark it as repeat/completed connector context, or repair the later route from the canonical source. |
| `btc_failure_repair_001` | A route is blindly discarded or preserved after a miss, extra segment, closure, or access change. | Plan repair before plan rejection | Update state, keep repeat/connector roles visible, and recertify the remaining menu. |
| `btc_failure_connector_001` | A route is credit-correct but field-wrong because a segment-order chain forces an unnecessary repeat after credit/access purpose is satisfied. | Connector provenance and no fake shortcuts | Treat the repeat as ordinary connector routing. Hard-fail field readiness when the connector graph proves a materially shorter legal path whose actual replacement geometry avoids the already-credited repeat; advisory-flag cases where no alternate path is proven. |
| `btc_failure_exception_001` | A reusable field-tested rule is buried in one route name, candidate id, package number, cue number, or public-label string branch. | Route-specific exceptions are heuristic debt | Keep the guard only as temporary protection, document the debt, and move the rule into reusable generator logic, local-reality config, certification manifests, or generic audits. |
| `btc_failure_field_day_001` | A calendar or optimizer proof is treated as a field guide even though selected loops are not linked to certified route cards, transfer costs, route-card GPX, and day-level validation. | Field-day layer over route cards | Build a day-level layer over certified route cards, show transfers and p75/p90 bounds, flag loops needing route-card promotion, and validate day-level GPX before publication. |
| `btc_failure_field_day_002` | The full route-card inventory audit is treated as the next route-decision queue even though the selected field days have a smaller, higher-impact blocker set. | Field-day scoped certification queue | Triage selected field-day loop blockers first, then keep unselected route-card failures as backlog unless they affect a backup, replacement, or redesign target. |
| `btc_failure_review_001` | A certified route card is preserved even though an accepted same-credit anchor materially reduces human footmiles or p75 time. | Certification is not non-dominance | Require `start_justification`, run exact-credit dominance review for single- and multi-segment routes, and block without a route/source-hashed waiver. |

Use `docs/BTC_CASES.md` for concrete observed instances and contrastive examples.
