# BTC Evidence Ladder

Use this order when evidence conflicts. Higher rows beat lower rows unless the task is explicitly retrospective and asks what an older planner believed at the time.

| Rank | Evidence | Use |
| ---: | --- | --- |
| 1 | Current-year official Boise Trails Challenge API/site data under `years/<year>/inputs/official/` | Official segments, trails, distance, direction, and challenge-window metrics. |
| 2 | Final public history API summaries for past years | Retrospective completion math. |
| 3 | Year-specific organizer change, closure, and land-manager notes | Legal access, routing constraints, and current trail availability. |
| 4 | Current condition reports, Ridge to Rivers map/signage, weather, and field observations | Day-of legality, mud, heat, and route readiness. |
| 5 | Personal Strava/API/export summaries and field-test logs | Pace, parking familiarity, route reconstruction, and actual field cost. |
| 6 | Supplemental connector data: Ridge to Rivers open data, OSM, DEM, and local GPX | Connector legality, effort, route shape, and navigation support. |
| 7 | Archived source code, 2025 local files, old architecture docs, and prior model summaries | Lineage and failure analysis only, unless the task is explicitly about historical behavior. |

Do not collapse evidence scope. A source can be authoritative for one question and weak for another. Strava can be strong for pace and parking history while still not being assumed as the official 2026 proof path.
